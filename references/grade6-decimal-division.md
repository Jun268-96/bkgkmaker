# Grade 6 decimal division scope

Use the current Korean 2022 revised national curriculum for elementary grades 5–6.

## Achievement standards

- `[6수01-14]`: Represent the quotient of natural-number division as a decimal.
- `[6수01-15]`: Explore the calculation principles of decimal division and calculate the result.

The published achievement-level material explicitly covers:

- `(natural number) ÷ (natural number)` with a decimal quotient
- `(decimal) ÷ (natural number)`
- `(natural number) ÷ (decimal)`
- `(decimal) ÷ (decimal)`
- checking plausibility using estimation at the advanced performance level

## Allowed item content

- Exact calculation with terminating decimal answers
- Place-value reasoning and multiplying dividend/divisor by the same power of ten
- Estimate-before-calculate and plausibility checks
- Comparing quotient size when the divisor is less than, equal to, or greater than 1
- Missing-number problems using the inverse relationship between multiplication and division
- Error analysis about decimal-point placement or scaling only one operand
- One-step, realistic sharing or measurement word problems
- Selecting an expression, equivalent expression, intermediate step, or explanation

## Scope guardrails

- Use nonnegative quantities and nonzero divisors.
- Prefer at most three decimal places in operands and quotients.
- Use exact finite quotients unless the prompt supplies an explicit grade-appropriate approximation rule.
- Exclude recurring-decimal notation, significant figures, negative-number division, algebraic manipulation, and rational-number theory.
- Avoid multi-step contexts whose main difficulty is unrelated reading or unit conversion.
- Do not infer a publisher's lesson sequence. Map every question directly to an achievement standard and content feature.
